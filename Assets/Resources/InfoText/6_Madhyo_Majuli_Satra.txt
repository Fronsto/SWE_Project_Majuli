Madhyo Majuli Satra is a revered religious site located in Majuli, the largest river island in Asia. This ancient site holds great significance among the followers of Vaishnavism, a sect of Hinduism. The Satra was established in the 16th century by the revered saint Srimanta Sankardeva, who was a major figure in the Bhakti movement in Assam.

The Satra is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, which includes traditional music, dance, and drama performances. It is also home to a large collection of ancient manuscripts and artifacts, providing a glimpse into the rich history and culture of the region.

Every year, thousands of devotees from all over India and abroad visit the Madhyo Majuli Satra to seek blessings and witness the grand celebration of festivals like Raas Purnima, which is marked by lively cultural events and performances. The serene ambiance, coupled with the spiritual aura of the Satra, make it a must-visit destination for those seeking solace and a deeper understanding of Indian culture and spirituality.
